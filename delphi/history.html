The History of Delphi

Journey through time in the cradle of Western civilization

3000 BCE

Early Settlement

Delphi's history begins with early settlements in the area, recognized for its natural beauty and significance in ancient Greek mythology.

8th Century BCE

Oracle of Delphi

The Sanctuary of Apollo was established, becoming home to the famous Oracle of Delphi, which played a crucial role in Greek religion and politics.

590 BCE

First Pythian Games

The first Pythian Games were held in honor of Apollo, becoming one of the major athletic festivals of ancient Greece.

4th Century BCE

Height of Influence

Delphi reached its peak as a cultural and religious center, with grand structures like the Temple of Apollo and the theater being constructed.

393 CE

Closure of the Oracle

The Oracle of Delphi was officially closed by Emperor Theodosius I, marking a significant decline in the site's influence.

Delphi: A Blend of History and Modernity
14th Century CE

Byzantine Period

During the Byzantine period, Delphi was largely abandoned but continued to hold cultural significance in the region.

19th Century

Archaeological Discoveries

Excavations in the 19th century revealed the extensive ruins of ancient Delphi, sparking interest in the site.

1987

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Delphi was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognized for its historical and cultural significance.

Present

Tourist Destination

Today, Delphi is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors to explore its ancient ruins and breathtaking landscapes.
