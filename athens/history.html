The History of Athens

Journey through time in the cradle of Western civilization

6000 BCE

First Settlements

The earliest human presence in Athens was marked by settlements around the Acropolis, taking advantage of the naturally defensible position and nearby water sources.

1400-1200 BCE

Mycenaean Period

Athens became a major center of Mycenaean civilization, with the Acropolis serving as a fortress for the local ruler. The city's first palace was built during this time.

508 BCE

Birth of Democracy

Cleisthenes established the world's first democratic system of government in Athens, introducing reforms that would influence political systems for millennia to come.

447-432 BCE

Golden Age

Under Pericles' leadership, Athens entered its Golden Age. The Parthenon was built, and the city became the center of art, philosophy, and literature in the ancient world.

86 BCE

Roman Conquest

Athens was captured by Roman forces under Sulla, marking the beginning of Roman rule. However, the city maintained its status as a cultural and educational center.

Athens Through the Ages
529 CE

End of Classical Era

Emperor Justinian closed the philosophical schools of Athens, marking the end of the classical era and the beginning of the Byzantine period.

1458 CE

Ottoman Rule

Athens fell to the Ottoman Empire, beginning a period of Ottoman rule that would last nearly four centuries.

1834

Modern Capital

After Greek independence, Athens was chosen as the capital of the modern Greek state, beginning its transformation into a modern metropolis.

2004

Olympic Return

Athens hosted the Summer Olympics, bringing the Games back to their spiritual home and showcasing the city's blend of ancient heritage and modern development.
