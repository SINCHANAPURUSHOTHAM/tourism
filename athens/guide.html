Athens Travel Guide

Everything you need to know for your Athens adventure

Getting to Athens

By Air

Athens International Airport (ATH) is the main gateway, located 20km east of the city center.

  • Metro Line 3 connects to city center (40 min)
  • X95 bus runs 24/7 to Syntagma Square
  • Taxi fare: approximately €38 (day) / €54 (night)

By Sea

Piraeus Port is the main maritime gateway to Athens.

  • Connected by Metro Line 1
  • Regular ferries from Greek islands
  • Cruise terminal facilities available

Where to Stay

Recommended Areas

  • Plaka - Historic center, close to attractions
  • Monastiraki - Shopping and nightlife
  • Kolonaki - Upscale area, boutique hotels
  • Koukaki - Local neighborhood, affordable options

Accommodation Types

Type Price Range (€/night)
Budget Hostels 15-30

Luxury Hotels 150+

Getting Around

Metro

3 lines covering major attractions and suburbs

  • Single ticket: €1.20 (90 minutes)
  • Daily pass: €4.10
  • Operating hours: 5:30 AM - 12:30 AM

Bus & Trolley

Extensive network throughout Athens

  • Same tickets as metro
  • Night buses on major routes
  • Airport buses run 24/7

Taxis

Abundant and relatively affordable

  • Starting fare: €1.29
  • Per kilometer: €0.74 (day) / €1.29 (night)
  • Use Beat app for reliable service

Practical Information

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (Mar-May): Mild weather, fewer crowds
  • Fall (Sep-Nov): Pleasant temperatures
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): Hot, peak tourist season
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Mild, occasional rain

Money Tips

  • ATMs widely available
  • Credit cards accepted in most places
  • Keep small cash for local markets
  • Tipping: 5-10% in restaurants

Safety & Emergency

Safety Tips

  • Watch for pickpockets in tourist areas
  • Keep copies of important documents
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-hailing apps
  • Stay hydrated during summer months

Emergency Numbers

  • Emergency: 112
  • Police: 100
  • Ambulance: 166
  • Tourist Police: 1571
